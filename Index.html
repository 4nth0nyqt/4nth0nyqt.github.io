Contemporary issues online

-What is contemporary issues?

Contemporary issues are current events or problems that are being dealt with in society and are relevant to the present time. These can include social, political, economic, and cultural issues that are facing the world today. Examples of contemporary issues might include climate change, income inequality, racial justice, immigration reform, and healthcare access.

    Internet addiction

    -What is Internet addiction?

    Internet addiction is characterized by an excessive or poorly controlled use of the Internet, which can lead to negative consequences in a person's life, such as problems with relationships, work, and health. Symptoms of internet addiction may include feeling a sense of euphoria while using the internet, needing to use the internet for longer periods of time in order to achieve the desired effect, and experiencing withdrawal symptoms when unable to access the internet. Internet addiction is not currently recognized as a diagnosable disorder by the American Psychiatric Association, but it is considered a significant problem by many mental health experts and researchers.

    The internet is a powerful tool, but balance is key. Don't let it consume you."

    • Cyberbullying

    -What is Cyberbullying?

    Cyberbullying is the use of digital technologies, such as the internet, social media, and messaging apps, to harass, intimidate, or harm someone. It can take many forms, such as sending threatening messages, spreading rumors, or posting embarrassing pictures or videos of someone without their consent. Cyberbullying can have serious consequences for the person being targeted, including feelings of fear, anxiety, depression, and even thoughts of suicide. It is important for people to be aware of cyberbullying and to take steps to prevent it or to intervene if they see it happening.
